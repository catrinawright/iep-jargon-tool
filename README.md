## What This Tool Does

This tool translates a documented community need — criminal record barriers to employment, housing, and community participation — into the legally binding administrative language that school administrators are already required to understand under federal law.

It is not a general IEP reference guide. It is a **compliance decision tree** built specifically for the intersection of transition-age special education programming (ages 18–22) and the reentry and records relief landscape in Illinois.

Each of the five scenarios presents a situation that arises in IEP meetings, transition planning sessions, or administrative decisions. The tool maps that situation to the specific legal terms it triggers, the plain-language meaning of those terms, the statutory obligation they create, and the procedural steps required to address them — including connections to legal aid resources such as New Leaf Illinois and Cabrini Green Legal Aid.

## Who It Is For

- **School administrators and LEA representatives** responsible for committing school resources at IEP meetings
- **Special education teachers and transition specialists** supporting IEP development for transition-age students
- **District compliance officers** reviewing IEPs for legal accuracy and procedural completeness
- **Nonprofit organizations and legal aid providers** working with families navigating both the IEP process and the criminal legal system

---

## The Five Scenarios

| # | Scenario | Primary Legal Triggers |
|---|----------|----------------------|
| 1 | Student's employment goal may be blocked by a criminal record | FAPE, Postsecondary Goals, Age-Appropriate Transition Assessment |
| 2 | Independent living goals have not addressed record barriers | Transition Services, Community Experiences, Independent Living |
| 3 | Administration is unsure whether expungement resources are within school scope | Participating Agency, WIOA Pre-ETS, Self-Advocacy Instruction |
| 4 | Student turns 18; rights transfer without legal status discussion | Transfer of Rights, Procedural Safeguards, Illinois Clean Slate Act |
| 5 | Criminal record raised as factor in placement discussion | LRE, FAPE, Prior Written Notice, Due Process |

## Features

- **Bilingual (English / Spanish)** with machine translation disclaimer for legal content
- **Read Aloud** on every screen including intake, scenarios, glossary, and references — with word-level synchronization and female voice preference
- **Per-entry glossary** with individual speak buttons for 21 legal terms across four organized groups
- **Highlight and annotate** text in four colors for notetaking
- **Print notes** — exports all highlights and self-check responses as a dated, formatted document
- **Quick View / Full Brief** toggle for meeting vs. deep-reference use
- **Intake routing** — two-question intake routes users to their most relevant scenario
- **Full keyboard navigation** and screen reader support (ARIA live regions, skip links, role attributes)
- **UDL-compliant** across all three principles: multiple means of representation, action and expression, and engagement

## Legal Framework

This tool is grounded in three federal frameworks:

- **34 CFR § 300.43** — Transition Services definition (IDEA)
- **34 CFR § 300.320(b)** — Transition IEP requirements
- **34 CFR § 361.48(a)** — WIOA Pre-Employment Transition Services

Illinois-specific resources referenced include the [Illinois Clean Slate Act](https://www.ilga.gov/), [New Leaf Illinois](https://newleafillinois.org/), [Cabrini Green Legal Aid](https://www.cgla.net/criminal-records), and [Illinois Legal Aid Online](https://www.illinoislegalaid.org/).

## How to Use

The tool is a single self-contained HTML file. No installation, server, or internet connection is required to run it (fonts load from Google Fonts if connected).

**Live version:** [catrinawright.github.io/iep-jargon-tool](https://catrinawright.github.io/iep-jargon-tool/)

To use locally: download `index.html` → open in Safari, Chrome, or Edge.
